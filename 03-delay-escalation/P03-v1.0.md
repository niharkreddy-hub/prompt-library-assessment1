## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Delayed stock directly causes failed customer order fulfilment, resulting in refunds, negative reviews, and customer churn. Manual escalation emails are inconsistently structured and often lack the commercial urgency needed to prompt rapid supplier response. Each delay incident currently requires 20–30 minutes of senior manager drafting time.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Inconsistent escalation structure — suppliers don't know what response is required
- No commercial impact framing — suppliers don't understand business cost of delay
- Senior manager time wasted on drafting rather than resolution strategy

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a supply chain manager. Write an urgent email to a supplier about a delivery delay. Ask them to explain the delay and give a new delivery date.  
**Output:** Short, generic email with no commercial urgency, no impact quantification, no structured response request, and no deadline. Would not drive rapid supplier action.  
**Observed effect:** Required full rewrite — no time saving. Tone insufficient for genuine escalation.  
**Lesson learned:** Escalation emails must quantify commercial impact, include a numbered response checklist, and set a hard response deadline.
