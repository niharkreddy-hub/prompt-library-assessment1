## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Delayed stock directly causes failed customer order fulfilment, resulting in refunds, negative reviews, and customer churn. Each delay incident costs SwiftFill an estimated $15–$40 per affected customer order in refund processing and reacquisition cost. Manual escalation emails lack commercial urgency and cost senior managers 20–30 minutes of drafting time per incident.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Inconsistent escalation structure — suppliers unclear on required response
- No commercial impact quantification — suppliers don't see business cost of delay
- Senior manager drafting time wasted — should be spent on resolution strategy
- No partial shipment option offered — missing opportunity to partially fulfil orders

## ⚡ Automation Potential

**Level:** Medium

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Repetitiveness | Per delay incident — variable but high-impact frequency |
| Data availability | All variables available from WMS and OMS |
| Human judgment needed | High — severity assessment and relationship context |
| Integration possibility | WMS delivery exception flag auto-trigger — Phase 2 |
| Estimated time saving | ~73% — from 30 min to ~8 min including manager review |

**Human-in-the-loop role:** Supply chain manager verifies all commercial impact figures before sending. Mandatory — no auto-send for escalation communications.

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| AI overstates impact if variables incorrect — damages credibility | High | Manager must verify units at risk and order count before sending |
| Tone too aggressive — may breach supplier contract terms | Medium | Legal review of template against key supplier SLAs annually |
| Sensitive commercial data (units, order volumes) in prompt | Medium | Restrict prompt access to supply chain staff only |
| 4-hour deadline may be unrealistic for international suppliers | Low | Apply timezone variable — adjust deadline for international partners |

**Overall risk rating:** MEDIUM — high human oversight requirement mitigates key risks.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a supply chain manager. Write an urgent email to a supplier about a delivery delay. Ask them to explain the delay and give a new delivery date.  
**Output:** Short generic email, no commercial impact, no structured response request, no deadline. Would not drive rapid supplier action.  
**Observed effect:** Required full rewrite — zero time saving.  
**Lesson learned:** Escalation emails must quantify commercial impact, include numbered response checklist, and set hard deadline.

---

### v1.1 — Added RACE role + commercial impact variables + 2-point response request
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added senior supply chain manager role, 7 placeholder variables including units at risk, firm urgent tone, and 2-point structured response request.  
**Output:** Strong urgency present. Units at risk quantified. Missing customer order count, 4-hour deadline, and partial shipment option.  
**Observed effect:** Editing time reduced from 30 min to ~10 min. Stronger but incomplete.  
**Lesson learned:** Commercial impact variables improve urgency dramatically. Need customer order count and hard deadline for complete escalation.

---

### v1.2 — Added customer orders count + 4-hour deadline + partial shipment + word limit ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added NUMBER_ORDERS variable, 4-hour response deadline with director escalation consequence, partial shipment request as 3rd response item, and 220-word limit.  
**Output:** Complete, commercially grounded escalation email. Supplier understands exact business impact and required response within defined timeframe.  
**Observed effect:** Drafting time reduced from 30 min to under 8 min — 73% saving. Output drives faster supplier response due to deadline and director escalation consequence.  
**Lesson learned:** Deadlines with consequences (escalate to account director) drive faster supplier action than open-ended requests. Partial shipment option recovers partial fulfilment even when full delivery is delayed.
