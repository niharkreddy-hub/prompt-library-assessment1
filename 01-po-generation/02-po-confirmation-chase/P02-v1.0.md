## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Unconfirmed POs create inventory planning uncertainty leading to stockouts and emergency procurement at premium cost. Staff spend significant time manually tracking outstanding POs across email threads with no standardised follow-up cadence, resulting in inconsistent response rates from suppliers.

**Pain points addressed:**
- No standardised follow-up cadence for unconfirmed POs
- Manual tracking of outstanding orders across multiple email threads
- Inconsistent tone — some staff too passive, others too aggressive

---

## ⚡ Automation Potential

**Level:** High

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Repetitiveness | Triggered for every unconfirmed PO at 72 hours |
| Data availability | PO number and dates available in WMS |
| Human judgment needed | Tone adjustment for sensitive supplier relationships |
| Integration possibility | Can auto-trigger from WMS confirmation status |
| Estimated time saving | ~90% — from 20 min to 2 min per chase email |

**Human-in-the-loop role:** Coordinator reviews draft and sends — adjusts tone for key supplier relationships if needed.

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Too aggressive tone damages supplier relationship | High | v1.0 has no tone instruction — fix in v1.1 |
| No confirmation checklist — vague ask | High | v1.0 doesn't specify what supplier must confirm |
| No deadline creates no urgency | Medium | Add response deadline in v1.1 |

**Overall risk rating:** HIGH — v1.0 output too vague and potentially damaging to supplier relationships.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a procurement coordinator. Write a follow-up email to a supplier who has not confirmed our purchase order. Ask them to confirm the order.  
**Output:** Very short, passive email with no subject line, no PO reference, no specific ask, and no deadline. Supplier would not know what action to take.  
**Observed effect:** Output unusable — required complete rewrite. No time saving.  
**Lesson learned:** Follow-up emails need a specific confirmation checklist, a deadline, a collaborative but firm tone, and all relevant PO reference data.
