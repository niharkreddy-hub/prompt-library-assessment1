## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.2 — current)
```
You are a procurement coordinator at an e-commerce fulfilment company. Draft a formal purchase order email to [SUPPLIER_NAME] requesting [PRODUCT_NAME] with a quantity of [QUANTITY] units at an agreed unit price of [UNIT_PRICE]. Include: PO reference number [PO_NUMBER], required delivery date [DELIVERY_DATE], delivery address [WAREHOUSE_ADDRESS], and payment terms of 30 days EOM. Format with a subject line, professional greeting, itemised order table, and a closing requesting written confirmation within 2 business days. Max 250 words. Do not include negotiation language or pricing flexibility.
```

| Placeholder | Source | Example |

| `[UNIT_PRICE]` | Agreed supplier contract | $2.40 AUD |
| `[PO_NUMBER]` | Auto-generated by WMS | PO-2024-0847 |
| `[DELIVERY_DATE]` | Procurement calendar | 15 November 2024 |
| `[WAREHOUSE_ADDRESS]` | Company records | Unit 4, 28 Logistics Drive, Derrimut VIC 3026 |

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** WMS inventory falls below reorder threshold and flags replenishment
- **Actor:** Procurement coordinator reviews AI draft and sends
- **Timing:** Same business day as trigger — target send within 4 hours
- **Next step:** Feeds into P02 (PO Confirmation Chase) if no supplier reply within 3 business days
```
[WMS reorder alert] → [P01 RUNS] → [Coordinator reviews] → [PO sent to supplier]
                                                                      ↓
                                              [No reply in 3 days] → [P02 triggered]
```

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Procurement staff spend 35–50 minutes per PO drafting manually. With 20–40 POs issued weekly, this represents up to 2,000 staff minutes per week on a single repetitive task. At a $45/hr fully-loaded staff cost, manual PO drafting costs SwiftFill up to $78,000 annually in administrative labour alone. Inconsistent formatting also causes supplier confusion, incorrect shipments, and costly dispute resolution.

**Pain points addressed:**
- High time cost per PO (35–50 min manual vs <5 min AI-assisted including review)
- Inconsistent format across different staff causing supplier errors
- Missing fields (payment terms, delivery address) cause re-work and delays

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Hallucination of incorrect quantities/prices if WMS variables are malformed | High | Validate all placeholder fields against WMS before prompt execution — null fields trigger alert, not prompt run |
| Supplier-specific terms missed if not in prompt variables | Medium | Maintain supplier exception list reviewed monthly by procurement manager |
| Over-reliance — staff approve without reading | Medium | Governance policy classifies all outputs as unreviewed drafts; review checkbox required before send |
| Sensitive pricing data in prompt outputs | Low | Restrict prompt access to procurement role; outputs stored in ERP only, not shared drives |

**Overall risk rating:** LOW-MEDIUM — risks are well-mitigated through validation gates and governance controls.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a procurement coordinator. Write a purchase order email to a supplier requesting stock. Include the order details and delivery date.  
**Output:** Generic short email, no subject line, no itemised table, casual tone, missing payment terms and delivery address. Required full manual rewrite.  
**Observed effect:** 25+ minutes editing required — no time saving over manual drafting.  
**Lesson learned:** Need RACE role, explicit structure, formal B2B tone, word limit, and exclusion rules.

---

**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added RACE role, 6 placeholder variables, formal B2B tone instruction, and basic output format.  
**Output:** Professional tone and structure present. Subject line included. Still missing itemised order table and payment terms.  
**Observed effect:** Editing time reduced from 25+ min to ~8–10 min. Usable with minor additions.  
**Lesson learned:** RACE role + tone = foundational. Table and payment terms must be explicitly instructed.

---

### v1.2 — Added itemised table + payment terms + word limit + exclusion ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added itemised order table instruction, 30-day EOM payment terms, unit price variable, 250-word limit, and explicit exclusion of negotiation language.  
**Output:** Complete, professional, business-ready PO email. No manual editing required before sending.  
**Observed effect:** Total drafting time reduced from 40 min (manual) to under 5 min including review — 87.5% time saving. Consistent output every run.  
**Lesson learned:** Format constraints (table, word limit, exclusion rules) convert a good output into a reliable, repeatable business tool.
