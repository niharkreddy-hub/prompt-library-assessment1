## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.1 — current)
```
You are a customer experience specialist at an e-commerce fulfilment company. Draft a proactive customer notification email for [CUSTOMER_NAME] regarding order [ORDER_NUMBER] placed on [ORDER_DATE]. Inform the customer that [PRODUCT_NAME] is temporarily out of stock due to a supplier delay and their order is on hold. Provide: (1) sincere apology, (2) estimated restock date: [RESTOCK_DATE], (3) three options — wait for restock, substitute with [ALTERNATIVE_PRODUCT] at no extra cost, or full refund. Include a direct reply contact email [SUPPORT_EMAIL]. Max 200 words. Tone: empathetic, transparent, and solution-focused. Do not reference the supplier name or cause of delay specifically.
```

**Placeholders to fill:**

| Placeholder | Source | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| `[CUSTOMER_NAME]` | OMS | Sarah Mitchell |
| `[ORDER_NUMBER]` | OMS | ORD-2024-18847 |
| `[ORDER_DATE]` | OMS | 12 November 2024 |
| `[PRODUCT_NAME]` | OMS | Bamboo Storage Baskets Set of 3 |
| `[RESTOCK_DATE]` | Supply chain team | Early December 2024 |
| `[ALTERNATIVE_PRODUCT]` | Product catalogue | Woven Seagrass Baskets Set of 3 |
| `[SUPPORT_EMAIL]` | Company records | support@swiftfill.com.au |

---

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** OMS fulfilment exception flagged due to supplier stock delay
- **Actor:** CX team verifies restock date and alternative product, then sends
- **Timing:** Within 4 hours of exception being flagged — before customer contacts support
- **Next step:** Customer selects resolution option — wait, substitute, or refund
```
[OMS exception] → [P08 RUNS] → [CX verifies restock + alternative] → [Email sent]
                                                                              ↓
                                                    [Customer selects resolution option]
                                                                              ↓
                                                         [OMS updated with resolution]
```

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Out-of-stock situations handled reactively generate an estimated 3–5 additional support contacts per incident and increase refund rates by 15–20% compared to proactive notification. Manual drafting takes 20–30 minutes per notification with inconsistent tone. SwiftFill processes approximately 40–60 fulfilment exceptions monthly — representing 800–1,800 minutes of CX staff time on a single communication type.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Reactive handling generates 3–5 additional support contacts per incident
- 20–30 min manual drafting — 800–1,800 min monthly across all exceptions
- Inconsistent tone damages customer relationships and brand perception
- Missing resolution options force customers to contact support unnecessarily

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Incorrect restock date destroys customer trust if not met | High | Only populate restock date when confirmed by supply chain — use 'early [MONTH]' if uncertain |
| Alternative product inappropriate for customer order history | Medium | Filter alternative product recommendation based on category match |
| Supplier name exclusion rule forgotten without constraint | Medium | Explicit exclusion in prompt constraints — not just guidelines |
| Customer contact data privacy — PII in prompt | Low | Restrict prompt access to CX staff only — GDPR/Privacy Act compliant storage |

**Overall risk rating:** LOW-MEDIUM — human verification gate for restock date is the critical control point.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a customer service agent. Write an email to a customer telling them their order is delayed because we don't have stock.  
**Output:** Blunt unsympathetic email, no resolution options, named supplier, no restock date. Would damage customer relationships if sent.  
**Observed effect:** Completely unusable — would increase complaints not reduce them.  
**Lesson learned:** Customer comms require empathetic tone, 3 resolution options, supplier exclusion rule, and solution-first framing. These are non-negotiable for brand protection.

---

### v1.1 — Added empathetic tone + 3 resolution options + supplier exclusion + word limit ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added CX specialist role, 7 placeholder variables, empathetic solution-focused tone, 3 explicit resolution options, supplier name exclusion constraint, support email, and 200-word limit.  
**Output:** Empathetic, transparent, actionable customer notification. Customer has clear path forward. No supplier named. No editing required.  
**Observed effect:** Drafting time reduced from 25 min to under 5 min — 80% saving. Resolution options reduce follow-up support contacts by estimated 60–70%.  
**Lesson learned:** Exclusion constraints (do not reference supplier) must be in the prompt body — not just in guidelines. Solution-first framing with 3 options dramatically reduces customer support escalation.
