- **Next step:** Warehouse team schedules receiving bay and allocates staff

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Delayed or inconsistent internal shipment alerts lead to unstaffed receiving bays, missed deliveries, and processing backlogs. Current informal messages omit tracking numbers, handling notes, and escalation contacts — causing preventable operational disruption. Each disrupted delivery costs approximately 1–2 hours of unplanned warehouse staff time.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Unstaffed receiving bays — estimated 1–2 hours disruption per missed alert
- Informal messages missing tracking numbers and handling requirements
- No escalation contact when delivery doesn't arrive as scheduled

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Incorrect delivery date cascades into wrong staffing | High | Cross-check against carrier tracking before internal send |
| Special handling field left blank causes handling errors | High | Make field mandatory in prompt trigger form — blank = alert |
| Future auto-send risks propagating incorrect data | Medium | Retain human review gate until portal integration fully tested |
| Over-reliance reduces staff situational awareness | Low | Monthly ops review of delivery performance data |

**Overall risk rating:** LOW-MEDIUM — human cross-check gate mitigates primary risks effectively.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a supply chain coordinator. Write an internal message to the warehouse team letting them know a supplier has confirmed a shipment is on its way.  
**Output:** Long conversational message, no action required, no tracking data, no escalation contact. Unusable as operational alert.  
**Observed effect:** Required complete rewrite — zero time saving.  
**Lesson learned:** Internal ops alerts must be brief, action-focused, include tracking data and escalation path. Word limit is essential.

---

### v1.1 — Added 9 variables + action required + word limit + escalation contact ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added 9 explicit placeholder variables, 3-point structured alert format (action required, special handling, escalation contact), 120-word limit, and operational direct tone instruction.  
**Output:** Concise, complete operational alert. Warehouse team has all information needed to prepare receiving bay. No editing required.  
**Observed effect:** Drafting time reduced from 15 min to under 1 min — 95% saving. Zero missed information in output.  
**Lesson learned:** Word limits are critical for operational alerts — they force the AI to prioritise essential information over padding. Mandatory fields prevent information gaps that cause operational errors.
