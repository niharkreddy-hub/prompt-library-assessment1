## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Ad-hoc RFQ emails are inconsistently structured, often missing key data points (MOQ, lead time, payment terms), resulting in incomplete quotes requiring multiple follow-up rounds. Running 5 RFQs previously took 2+ hours — adding days to the procurement cycle and delaying stock availability.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Inconsistent RFQ structure — suppliers return incomparable quotes
- Missing data points require multiple follow-up rounds per supplier
- 2+ hours to send RFQs to 5 suppliers manually

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Output implies purchase commitment — legal risk | High | Fix in v1.1 — add explicit non-committal tone instruction |
| Missing 5-point data request — incomplete quotes returned | High | Fix in v1.1 — add structured 5-point information request |
| No quote deadline | Medium | Fix in v1.1 — add quote response deadline |

**Overall risk rating:** HIGH — v1.0 could imply purchase commitment and returns incomplete quotes.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a procurement manager. Write an email to a supplier asking for a quote on a product we need.  
**Output:** Short email sounding like a purchase order — implied commitment to buy. No specific data points requested, no deadline. Legally risky and returns incomparable quotes.  
**Observed effect:** Required complete rewrite — legally risky output.  
**Lesson learned:** RFQ emails must explicitly state non-commitment, request 5 specific data points, and include a quote deadline. Missing any of these makes quotes incomparable and legally ambiguous.
