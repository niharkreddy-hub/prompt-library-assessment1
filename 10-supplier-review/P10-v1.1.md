## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.1 — current)
```
You are a supplier relationship manager preparing for a monthly review meeting with [SUPPLIER_NAME]. Using the following data, generate a structured relationship review summary: Average On-Time Delivery: [OTD_AVG]% | Total POs Issued: [TOTAL_POS] | Discrepancies: [DISC_COUNT] (value: $[DISC_VALUE] AUD) | Average Response Time: [RESP_TIME] hours | Contract Renewal Date: [RENEWAL_DATE]. Structure the summary as: (1) Relationship Health Score: Strong / Satisfactory / At Risk — with one-sentence rationale, (2) Top 2 Strengths, (3) Top 2 Areas for Improvement, (4) Recommended agenda items for review meeting (max 3), (5) Recommended contract action: Renew / Renegotiate / Review. Max 280 words. Tone: analytical and objective.
```

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** Last business day of each month — data compiled from WMS and procurement records
- **Actor:** Supplier relationship manager reviews summary and uses it to build meeting agenda
- **Timing:** Generated end of month — meeting scheduled within first week of following month
- **Next step:** Summary drives meeting agenda; contract action recommendation escalated to procurement director for sign-off
```
[Month end data] → [P10 RUNS] → [Manager reviews + adds qualitative notes]
                                                ↓

                                                ↓
                                [Supplier review meeting held]
                                                ↓
                            [Contract action → Director sign-off]
```

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Monthly supplier reviews are unstructured and reactive, lacking consistent data-driven framing. Procurement managers spend 60–90 minutes manually compiling performance data before each review. Without a health score or contract action recommendation, renewal decisions are made on intuition rather than evidence — increasing commercial and operational risk. Across 10 active suppliers, manual preparation costs SwiftFill 600–900 minutes monthly — $450–$675 in staff time per month.

**Pain points addressed:**
- 60–90 min manual preparation per supplier — $450–$675 monthly across 10 suppliers
- Unstructured reviews miss critical performance trends
- Contract renewal decisions made without evidence base — commercial risk
- No health score means at-risk relationships not identified until disruption occurs

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Health score misrepresents complex supplier dynamics if based on quantitative data only | High | Manager adds qualitative notes before using summary in meetings — mandatory field |
| Contract action recommendations carry legal and commercial weight | High | Label all outputs as AI-generated drafts — procurement director sign-off required before action |
| Supplier performance data is commercially sensitive | Medium | Restrict prompt access to authorised procurement staff — outputs stored in secure ERP only |
| Consistent format may mask nuanced long-term relationship dynamics | Low | Manager override option — qualitative context section added to all summaries |

**Overall risk rating:** MEDIUM — significant human oversight requirements appropriately managed through governance controls and director sign-off gate.

## 🔄 Version History

### v1.0 — Initial draft
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Prompt:** You are a supplier manager. Write a monthly review summary for a supplier. Talk about how they performed this month.  
**Output:** Generic narrative with fabricated metrics, no health score, no agenda items, no contract recommendation. Completely unusable — hallucinated performance data throughout.  
**Observed effect:** Required complete rewrite — fabricated data dangerous for contract decisions.  
**Lesson learned:** Review summaries MUST include structured data inputs — identical lesson to P07. AI fabricates metrics without grounding data. Health score, agenda items, and contract recommendation must be explicitly required in prompt structure.

---

### v1.1 — Added 7 data inputs + 5-section structure + health score + contract recommendation ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added supplier relationship manager RACE role, 7 structured data input variables from WMS and contract system, 5-section output structure (health score, strengths, improvements, agenda items, contract action), analytical objective tone instruction, and 280-word limit.  
**Output:** Data-grounded, structured monthly review summary. Health score present with rationale. Contract action recommendation included. Meeting agenda items specific and actionable. No hallucinated metrics. No editing required.  
**Observed effect:** Preparation time reduced from 75 min to ~22 min per supplier — 70% saving. Health score enables proactive identification of at-risk relationships before disruption occurs. Contract recommendations provide evidence base for renewal decisions.  
**Lesson learned:** Structured data inputs eliminate hallucination in reporting prompts — consistent finding across P07 and P10. Analytical tone instruction prevents overly diplomatic outputs that obscure genuine performance issues. Director sign-off gate is essential for outputs with contract implications.
