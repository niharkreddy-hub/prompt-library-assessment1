You are a procurement coordinator at an e-commerce fulfilment company. Write a professional follow-up email to [SUPPLIER_NAME] regarding Purchase Order [PO_NUMBER] issued on [PO_DATE], which has not yet been confirmed. The required delivery date is [DELIVERY_DATE]. Politely but firmly request written confirmation of order receipt and estimated dispatch date. Maintain a professional and collaborative tone throughout. Include a subject line.

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** 3 business days pass with no supplier confirmation of PO
- **Actor:** Procurement coordinator reviews and sends
- **Timing:** Automatically flagged by WMS at 72-hour mark
- **Next step:** If no reply after 1 business day, triggers P03 escalation
```
[PO sent via P01] → [3 days no reply] → [P02 RUNS] → [Coordinator reviews & sends]
                                                                ↓
                                          [Still no reply] → [P03 triggered]
```

---

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Unconfirmed POs create inventory planning uncertainty leading to stockouts and emergency procurement at premium cost. Staff spend significant time manually tracking outstanding POs with no standardised follow-up cadence, resulting in inconsistent supplier response rates and delayed fulfilment.

**Pain points addressed:**
- No standardised follow-up cadence for unconfirmed POs
- Manual tracking across multiple email threads
- Inconsistent tone causing supplier relationship friction

---

## ⚡ Automation Potential

**Level:** High

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Repetitiveness | Triggered for every unconfirmed PO at 72 hours |
| Data availability | PO data available directly from WMS |
| Human judgment needed | Tone adjustment for key supplier relationships |
| Integration possibility | Auto-trigger from WMS confirmation status field |
| Estimated time saving | ~90% — from 20 min to 2 min per chase email |

**Human-in-the-loop role:** Coordinator reviews draft and sends — may adjust tone for sensitive relationships.
