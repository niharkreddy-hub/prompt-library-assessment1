## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.2 — current)
```
You are a procurement coordinator at an e-commerce fulfilment company. Write a professional follow-up email to [SUPPLIER_NAME] regarding Purchase Order [PO_NUMBER] issued on [PO_DATE], which has not yet been confirmed. The required delivery date is [DELIVERY_DATE]. Politely but firmly request written confirmation of: (1) order receipt, (2) confirmed quantity, (3) estimated dispatch date. Note that failure to confirm within 1 business day may require us to source from an alternative supplier. Maintain a professional and collaborative tone throughout. Max 180 words. Include a subject line.
```

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** 3 business days pass with no supplier confirmation of PO
- **Actor:** Procurement coordinator reviews and sends
- **Timing:** Automatically flagged by WMS at 72-hour mark
- **Next step:** If no reply after 1 business day, triggers P03 escalation
```
[PO sent via P01] → [3 days no reply] → [P02 RUNS] → [Coordinator reviews & sends]
                                                                ↓
                                          [Still no reply] → [P03 triggered]
```

---

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Unconfirmed POs create inventory planning uncertainty leading to stockouts and emergency procurement at premium cost. Staff spend significant time manually tracking outstanding POs with no standardised follow-up cadence, resulting in inconsistent supplier response rates and delayed fulfilment planning.

**Pain points addressed:**
- No standardised follow-up cadence for unconfirmed POs (20 min manual per chase)
- Manual tracking across multiple email threads with no visibility
- Inconsistent tone — some staff too passive, others too aggressive with suppliers

---

## ⚡ Automation Potential

**Level:** High

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Repetitiveness | Triggered for every unconfirmed PO at 72 hours |
| Data availability | All PO data available directly from WMS |
| Human judgment needed | Tone adjustment for key/sensitive supplier relationships |
| Integration possibility | Auto-trigger from WMS confirmation status field — Phase 2 |
| Estimated time saving | ~90% — from 20 min to under 2 min per chase email |

**Human-in-the-loop role:** Coordinator reviews draft, adjusts tone for key suppliers if needed, and sends. No drafting required.

---

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Automated send without review could damage supplier relationships | High | Mandatory human review gate before all sends — no auto-send |
| Incorrect PO date from WMS timestamp format errors | Medium | Validate date format in WMS integration before populating variable |
| Escalation language too firm for long-term strategic partners | Medium | Segment suppliers by tier — apply tone modifier for Tier 1 partners |
| Over-reliance on AI may reduce staff awareness of supplier nuances | Low | Quarterly supplier relationship review by procurement manager |

**Overall risk rating:** LOW-MEDIUM — well-mitigated through human review gate and supplier tier segmentation.

### v1.2 — Added 3-point checklist + 1-day deadline + word limit ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added explicit 3-point confirmation checklist (receipt, quantity, dispatch date), 1 business day response deadline with alternative supplier consequence, and 180-word limit.  
**Output:** Clear, professional, actionable confirmation chase. Supplier knows exactly what to confirm and by when. No editing required.  
**Observed effect:** Editing time reduced from 20 min (manual) to under 2 min — 90% time saving. Supplier response rate improved due to specific action request.  
**Lesson learned:** Specific numbered checklists and deadlines drive faster supplier action. Consequence language ('may source from alternative') creates urgency without aggression.
