## 📌 Prompt Text (v1.2 — current)
```
You are a warehouse operations manager at an e-commerce fulfilment company. Draft a formal stock discrepancy notification to [SUPPLIER_NAME] regarding delivery received on [DELIVERY_DATE] under PO [PO_NUMBER]. Our warehouse team recorded receipt of [ACTUAL_UNITS] units against [ORDERED_UNITS] units ordered — a shortfall of [SHORTFALL_UNITS] units valued at approximately [SHORTFALL_VALUE] AUD. Request: (1) credit note or replacement shipment within 5 business days, (2) written acknowledgement of discrepancy, (3) updated delivery confirmation. Reference our supplier agreement clause [CLAUSE_NUMBER] if applicable. Maintain a factual, professional tone. Max 230 words. Do not include apology language on our behalf.
```

**Placeholders to fill:**

| Placeholder | Source | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| `[SUPPLIER_NAME]` | Supplier database | AusPack Supplies Pty Ltd |
| `[DELIVERY_DATE]` | Warehouse receiving log | 18 November 2024 |
| `[PO_NUMBER]` | WMS | PO-2024-0847 |
| `[ACTUAL_UNITS]` | Warehouse receiving log | 463 units |
| `[ORDERED_UNITS]` | PO record | 500 units |
| `[SHORTFALL_UNITS]` | Calculated | 37 units |
| `[SHORTFALL_VALUE]` | PO unit price × shortfall | $88.80 AUD |
| `[CLAUSE_NUMBER]` | Supplier agreement | Clause 4.2 |

## 🏢 Intended Workflow or Task

- **Trigger:** Warehouse receiving team identifies shortfall during goods-in verification
- **Actor:** Warehouse manager reviews, attaches receiving docs, and sends within 24 hours
- **Timing:** Same day as goods-in verification — target send within 4 hours of identification
- **Next step:** Supplier issues credit note or arranges replacement within 5 business days

## ❗ Problem Being Solved

Stock discrepancies cost SwiftFill an estimated $8,000–$15,000 monthly in unrecovered shortfalls across all suppliers. Without standardised dispute letters, claims are poorly documented, delayed, or abandoned entirely. Manual drafting takes 25–35 minutes per incident at a fully-loaded cost of $45/hr — adding $19–$26 in administrative cost per dispute on top of the shortfall value itself.

**Pain points addressed:**
- Unrecovered shortfall revenue — estimated $8,000–$15,000 monthly
- Inconsistent dispute letters — informal claims frequently ignored by suppliers
- No contract clause reference — disputes lack legal grounding
- Apology language in current emails weakens SwiftFill's claim position

## ⚠️ Risks and Limitations

| Risk | Level | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Incorrect shortfall value if unit price outdated | High | Pull unit price from PO record at prompt execution — not from memory |
| Clause reference not applicable to all suppliers | Medium | Make clause variable conditional — flag when not in supplier agreement |
| Sending for trivial shortfalls damages relationships | Medium | Set minimum threshold: >3 units OR >$50 AUD before prompt triggers |
| Dispute letter used in legal proceedings without review | Low | Label all outputs as drafts — require manager sign-off before send |

**Overall risk rating:** LOW-MEDIUM — well-mitigated through validation gates and threshold controls.

**Output:** Very informal short email, no financial figures, no deadline, no documentation reference. Unusable for formal dispute.

**Lesson learned:** Financial quantification strengthens dispute position. Contract clause reference and apology exclusion needed for maximum legal weight.

---

### v1.2 — Added clause reference + 3-point resolution request + apology exclusion + word limit ✅ Current
**Date:** 31 March 2026  
**Change:** Added clause reference variable, expanded to 3-point resolution request (credit note, acknowledgement, updated delivery), apology exclusion rule, and 230-word limit.  
**Output:** Formally grounded, legally appropriate dispute letter. No manual editing required.  
**Observed effect:** Editing time reduced from 30 min to under 6 min — 80% saving. Supplier resolution rate improved due to formal clause reference and structured demand.  
**Lesson learned:** Exclusion rules (no apology language) are as important as inclusions. Clause references signal legal seriousness and improve supplier compliance rates.
